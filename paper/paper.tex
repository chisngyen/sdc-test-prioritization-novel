\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{RoadFury}, a test prioritization tool for the ICST~2026 SDC Testing Competition~\cite{b8}.
Existing tools reduce road geometry to aggregate statistics, discarding sequential structure.
RoadFury preserves the full geometric sequence: a 10-channel feature matrix encoding curvature, heading, jerk, and positional context at every road point, processed by a Pre-LN Transformer Encoder~\cite{b11} with Stochastic Weight Averaging (SWA)~\cite{b6}.
Deployed as a gRPC service, RoadFury achieves APFD~=~\textbf{0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012}, surpassing the prior best of 0.781.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
test prioritization, self-driving cars, Transformer, stochastic weight averaging
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\section{Introduction}

Testing self-driving cars in simulators like BeamNG.tech is essential but costly~\cite{b1,b2}.
\emph{Test prioritization} reorders tests so failure-revealing cases run first, measured by APFD~\cite{b4}:
$\text{APFD}(\pi) = 1 - \sum_{i=1}^{m} TF_i/(n{\cdot}m) + 1/(2n)$,
where $n$ is total tests, $m$ failing tests, and $TF_i$ the rank of the $i$-th fault.
Existing tools such as SDC-Scissor~\cite{b1,b2} compute three aggregate statistics per road and use a feedforward network, discarding the \emph{sequential structure} that makes a road dangerous.
\textbf{RoadFury} addresses this by preserving the full ordered sequence with a Transformer Encoder and SWA~\cite{b6}.

\section{Approach}

\subsection{Feature Extraction}

Each test is a variable-length sequence of 2D road points.
We resample to $L{=}197$ points and extract 10 intrinsic geometry channels (Table~\ref{tab:features}), yielding a $(197{\times}10)$ translation- and rotation-invariant feature matrix, z-normalized with training-set statistics.

\caption{10-Channel Road Geometry Features}

\begin{center}

{\small\begin{tabular}{cll}

\textbf{Ch.} & \textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
$f_0$ & Segment length    & $\|p_{i+1}-p_i\|_2$ \\
$f_1$ & Abs.\ angle change & $|\Delta\theta_i|$ \\
$f_2$ & Menger curvature  & $\kappa_i=4A_i/(a_i b_i c_i)$ \\
$f_3$ & Curvature jerk    & $\Delta\kappa_i$ \\

$f_6$ & Heading $\cos$    & $\cos(\theta_i)$ \\

\subsection{RoadTransformer Architecture}

Fig.~\ref{fig:arch} illustrates the full RoadFury pipeline, which consists of four stages.
\textbf{(1)~Road Resampling:} The variable-length sequence of 2D control points defining each road is uniformly resampled to a fixed length of $L{=}197$ points, ensuring consistent input dimensionality.
\textbf{(2)~Feature Encoding:} At each resampled point, the 10 geometry channels from Table~\ref{tab:features} are computed, producing a $(197{\times}10)$ feature matrix that is subsequently z-normalized using training-set statistics to be translation- and rotation-invariant.
\textbf{(3)~Transformer Encoding:} A linear projection maps each 10-dim point to 128-dim with LayerNorm and GELU activation. A learnable \texttt{[CLS]} token is prepended and sinusoidal positional embeddings are added. A 4-layer Pre-LN Transformer Encoder~\cite{b11} ($d{=}128$, 8 heads, FFN$=$512, dropout$=$0.1) processes the resulting 198 tokens, enabling global self-attention over the entire road geometry.
\textbf{(4)~Failure Prediction:} The \texttt{[CLS]} output passes through an MLP head (two fully-connected layers with GELU and dropout) producing a failure probability $\hat{y}$ used for prioritization ranking (829K parameters total).


\subsection{Training and Deployment}

We train on 28,804 SensoDat~\cite{b7} samples with AdamW (lr$=$5$\times$10$^{-4}$, weight decay$=$10$^{-3}$) and BCE for 75 epochs.
SWA~\cite{b6} averages weights from epoch 50--75, finding flatter optima.
The tool is a Docker-containerized gRPC service with endpoints \texttt{Name}, \texttt{Initialize}, and \texttt{Prioritize}.

\section{Evaluation}

We evaluate on the 956-test competition set, averaging over 30 random trials.
Table~\ref{tab:results} compares four paradigms we explored.

Language models and graph networks discard sequential dynamics; CNNs lose numeric precision.
RoadFury's Transformer learns which curvature \emph{transitions} predict failures.
Among seven modifications tested (focal loss~\cite{b9}, mixup, stochastic depth, TTA, etc.), only SWA consistently improved results.

\section{Conclusion}

RoadFury demonstrates that preserving sequential road geometry, rather than aggregating it, is the key to effective test prioritization.
A Pre-LN Transformer with SWA achieves APFD\,=\,0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012, establishing a new state of the art for the ICST~2026 SDC Competition.
The tool is publicly available at \url{https://github.com/chisngyen/sdc-test-prioritization-novel}.
