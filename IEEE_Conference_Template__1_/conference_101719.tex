\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{RoadFury}, a test prioritization tool for simulation-based regression testing of self-driving cars. In BeamNG.tech simulations, executing large test suites is prohibitively expensive; prioritization reorders tests so that failure-revealing cases execute first. RoadFury extracts a 10-channel sequential feature matrix from road geometry---curvature, heading sin/cos, curvature jerk, curvature acceleration, local variability, and positional encodings---and processes it with a Pre-LN Transformer Encoder with learnable \texttt{[CLS]} token, trained with Stochastic Weight Averaging (SWA) for robust generalization. Deployed as a Docker-containerized gRPC service for the ICST~2026 SDC Testing Competition~\cite{b8}, RoadFury achieves APFD~=~\textbf{0.8042\,$\pm$\,0.0120}, surpassing ITEP4SDC (0.7812) and random ordering (0.4930).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Simulation-based testing of SDCs in high-fidelity simulators (e.g., BeamNG.tech) is indispensable for validation yet expensive~\cite{b1,b2}: individual tests run for tens of seconds to minutes. The rapid ascent of AI agents in autonomous navigation~\cite{b16} widens this gap further. \emph{Test prioritization} addresses this by finding a permutation $\pi$ that maximizes early fault detection, measured by APFD~\cite{b4}:
\vspace{-3pt}
\begin{equation}
\text{APFD}(\pi) = 1 - \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{m} TF_i}{n \cdot m} + \frac{1}{2n}
\end{equation}
where $n$ is total tests, $m$ failing tests, $TF_i$ the rank of the $i$-th fault (1.0: all failures first; 0.5: random).

Prior tools---SDC-Scissor~\cite{b2} and ITEP4SDC---use 3 hand-crafted features (segment length, angle change, curvature) in feedforward networks, discarding the \emph{sequential ordering} of road geometry. \textbf{RoadFury} addresses this gap with: (1)~a \emph{10-channel geometric feature representation} encoding higher-order curvature dynamics and positional context; and (2)~a \emph{Transformer Encoder with SWA} capturing long-range inter-point dependencies. A systematic evaluation~\cite{b15} confirms Transformers consistently outperform classical classifiers on sequential feature inputs; analogous early-stage ML prediction of AI-artifact outcomes is validated in related work~\cite{b14}. SDC-Scissor~\cite{b1,b2} pioneered road-feature ML; Yoo and Harman~\cite{b4} survey regression testing; Izmailov et al.~\cite{b6} show SWA finds wider optima. To identify the optimal paradigm, we systematically compare visual CNNs, GNNs, zero-shot LLMs, and sequential Transformer encoding---empirically converging on the latter.

\section{Approach}

\subsection{Road Normalization and Feature Extraction}

Each test case is a variable-length sequence of 2D road control points. We resample all sequences to $L{=}197$ points via linear interpolation and extract 10 intrinsic geometric channels (Table~\ref{tab:features}), yielding a $(197{\times}10)$ translation- and rotation-invariant feature matrix, z-normalized with training-set statistics.

\textbf{Ch.} & \textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule

$f_1$ & Abs.\ angle change & $|\Delta\theta_i|$ \\
$f_2$ & Menger curvature  & $\kappa_i=4A_i/(a_i b_i c_i)$, Heron's formula \\
$f_3$ & Curvature jerk    & $\Delta\kappa_i$, 1\textsuperscript{st} derivative of $\kappa$ \\
$f_4$ & Cum.\ dist.\ norm & $\textstyle\sum_{j<i}\!\|p_{j+1}{-}p_j\|/d_\text{tot}\in[0,1]$ \\
$f_5$ & Heading $\sin$    & $\sin(\theta_i)$, $\theta_i{=}\text{atan2}(\Delta y,\Delta x)$ \\

\subsection{RoadTransformer Architecture}

The $(197{\times}10)$ matrix passes through four stages (Fig.~\ref{fig:arch}).
\textbf{(1+2) Project + embed:} $\mathbf{h}_i{=}\text{GELU}(\text{LN}(\mathbf{W}_p\mathbf{f}_i{+}\mathbf{b}_p))$, projecting 10$\to$128; learnable $\texttt{[CLS]}\in\mathbb{R}^{128}$ prepended; positional embeddings added (len.~198).

\textbf{(3) Pre-LN Transformer Encoder~\cite{b11}:} 4 layers, 8 heads, FFN 512, GELU, dropout 0.1. Pre-LN residual updates: $\mathbf{Z}'{=}\mathbf{Z}^{(l)}{+}\mathrm{MHA}(\mathrm{LN}(\mathbf{Z}^{(l)}))$; $\mathbf{Z}^{(l+1)}{=}\mathbf{Z}'{+}\mathrm{FFN}(\mathrm{LN}(\mathbf{Z}'))$. CLS output $\to$ LN $\to$ Linear(128$\to$64) $\to$ GELU $\to$ Drop(0.2) $\to$ Linear(64$\to$1) $\to$ Sigmoid. Output $\hat{y}{\in}[0,1]$ is failure probability (${\sim}400$K params total).

\subsection{Training (SWA) and Deployment}

Adam (lr\,=\,1e-3, BCE), 75 epochs on 28,804 samples. SWA~\cite{b6} starts at epoch\,50---averaging weights over the final 25 epochs finds a flatter loss basin, improving robustness to unseen road geometries. The SWA checkpoint (\texttt{roadfury\_best.pt}) stores the averaged state dict and per-channel normalization statistics.

At inference, $n$ tests are batched $(n,10,197)$, scored in one forward pass, and sorted by descending $\hat{y}$. The service exposes \texttt{Name()}, \texttt{Initialize(oracle)}, and \texttt{Prioritize(tests)}$\to$scores via gRPC, containerized on port 4545.

\section{Evaluation}

\textbf{Dataset.} SensoDat~\cite{b7}: 36,006 labeled BeamNG tests, split 80/20 (28,804 train / 7,202 val); 956-test competition set is the held-out target.

\textbf{Baselines.} Beyond Random and ITEP4SDC (prior SoA, ONNX MLP, 3 features), we evaluate three additional paradigms on the same 30-trial protocol (Table~\ref{tab:results}): (i)~\textbf{LLM zero-shot}---Qwen2.5-7B~\cite{b16} in 4-bit quantization scores each road from a textual curvature summary; (ii)~\textbf{GNN}---road points as a graph with sequence and spatial KNN edges (3-layer GCN~\cite{b21}); (iii)~\textbf{road image CNN}---road polylines rendered to $224{\times}224$ px images, features extracted by ImageNet-pretrained CNNs~\cite{b5} fed into XGBoost.

\caption{Multi-Paradigm Comparison (956 comp.\ tests, 30 trials)}
\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{center}{\small
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{3pt}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
\textbf{Method} & \textbf{APFD} & \textbf{Paradigm} \\
\midrule
Random                   & 0.493              & -- \\
LLM zero-shot            & 0.487\,$\pm$\,0.019 & Qwen2.5-7B, 4-bit \\

ResNet50${\to}$XGB       & 0.572\,$\pm$\,0.013 & Road image CNN \\

ExtendedCNN-10ch         & 0.768\,$\pm$\,0.014 & 10-ch CNN, 28K data \\
ITEP4SDC                 & 0.781              & ONNX MLP, 3-feat \\

Table~\ref{tab:results} reveals a clear \emph{paradigm hierarchy}. Zero-shot LLMs score below random (0.487\,$\pm$\,0.019): language models cannot reliably reason about curvature thresholds. GNNs reach 0.533\,$\pm$\,0.025---spatial connectivity misses the sequential ordering critical for vehicle dynamics. Visual CNNs plateau at 0.673 even fused with 44 handcrafted features. The \emph{ExtendedCNN-10ch}---our 10-channel featuriser on a deeper CNN trained with 28K SensoDat data---reaches 0.768\,$\pm$\,0.014, showing \emph{feature quality} matters more than scale. \textbf{RoadFury} achieves \textbf{0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012}: +2.9\% over ITEP4SDC, +4.6\% over ExtendedCNN-10ch, +63\% over random.

\textbf{Ablation (single-factor).} Isolating each modification over the base Pre-LN Transformer (0.790): Multi-Scale Conv Stem: 0.767; DropPath~\cite{b20}: 0.768; Triple Pool: 0.768; Mixup~\cite{b19}: 0.773; TTA: 0.770; Focal Loss~\cite{b18} ($\gamma$=2.0): 0.782\,$\pm$\,0.014; \textbf{SWA}~\cite{b6}: \textbf{0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012}. SWA is the sole consistently beneficial modification (+0.014); all structural variants hurt the base Pre-LN design, which is already well-matched to sequential road geometry.

%=====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}

\textbf{RoadFury} achieves APFD\,=\,0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012 on the ICST~2026 SDC Competition benchmark---surpassing all four explored paradigms: zero-shot LLMs (0.487, \emph{below} random), GNNs (0.533), visual CNNs (0.673), and the prior SoA ITEP4SDC (0.781). Our systematic exploration empirically validates that sequential geometric encoding---not visual, graph, or language representations---is the dominant inductive bias for SDC test prioritization, and that SWA over a Pre-LN Transformer is the most effective training strategy. Future work targets online oracle adaptation and multi-scale SWA scheduling. Tool: \url{https://github.com/chisngyen/sdc-test-prioritization-novel}.
