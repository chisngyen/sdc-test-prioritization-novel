\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{RoadFury}, a test prioritization tool for simulation-based regression testing of self-driving cars. In BeamNG.tech simulations, executing large test suites is prohibitively expensive; prioritization reorders tests so that failure-revealing cases execute first. RoadFury extracts a 10-channel sequential feature matrix from road geometry---curvature, heading sin/cos, curvature jerk, curvature acceleration, local variability, and positional encodings---and processes it with a Pre-LN Transformer Encoder with learnable \texttt{[CLS]} token, trained with Stochastic Weight Averaging (SWA) for robust generalization. Deployed as a Docker-containerized gRPC service for the ICST~2026 SDC Testing Competition~\cite{b8}, RoadFury achieves APFD~=~\textbf{0.8042\,$\pm$\,0.0120}, surpassing ITEP4SDC (0.7812) and random ordering (0.4930).
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
test prioritization, self-driving cars, Transformer, stochastic weight averaging, road geometry

\section{Introduction}

Simulation-based testing of SDCs in high-fidelity simulators (e.g., BeamNG.tech) is indispensable for validation yet expensive~\cite{b1,b2}: individual tests run for tens of seconds to minutes. The rapid ascent of AI agents in autonomous navigation~\cite{b16} widens this gap further. \emph{Test prioritization} addresses this by finding a permutation $\pi$ that maximizes early fault detection, measured by APFD~\cite{b4}:
\vspace{-3pt}
\begin{equation}
\text{APFD}(\pi) = 1 - \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{m} TF_i}{n \cdot m} + \frac{1}{2n}
\end{equation}
where $n$ is total tests, $m$ failing tests, $TF_i$ the rank of the $i$-th fault (1.0: all failures first; 0.5: random).

Prior tools---SDC-Scissor~\cite{b2} and ITEP4SDC---use 3 hand-crafted features (segment length, angle change, curvature) in feedforward networks, discarding the \emph{sequential ordering} of road geometry. \textbf{RoadFury} addresses this gap with: (1)~a \emph{10-channel geometric feature representation} encoding higher-order curvature dynamics and positional context; and (2)~a \emph{Transformer Encoder with SWA} capturing long-range inter-point dependencies. A systematic evaluation~\cite{b15} confirms Transformers consistently outperform classical classifiers on sequential feature inputs; analogous early-stage ML prediction of AI-artifact outcomes is validated in related work~\cite{b14}. SDC-Scissor~\cite{b1,b2} pioneered road-feature ML for test selection; Yoo and Harman~\cite{b4} survey regression testing strategies; Izmailov et al.~\cite{b6} show SWA finds wider optima; broader contextual enrichment benefits are shown in~\cite{b17}.

\section{Approach}

\subsection{Road Normalization and Feature Extraction}

Each test case is a variable-length sequence of 2D road control points. We resample all sequences to $L{=}197$ points via linear interpolation and extract 10 intrinsic geometric channels (Table~\ref{tab:features}), yielding a $(197{\times}10)$ translation- and rotation-invariant feature matrix, z-normalized with training-set statistics.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{10-Channel Road Geometry Features}
\begin{center}
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{3pt}
{\small\begin{tabular}{cll}
\toprule
\textbf{Ch.} & \textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
$f_0$ & Segment length    & $\|p_{i+1}-p_i\|_2$ \\
$f_1$ & Abs.\ angle change & $|\Delta\theta_i|$ \\
$f_2$ & Menger curvature  & $\kappa_i=4A_i/(a_i b_i c_i)$, Heron's formula \\
$f_3$ & Curvature jerk    & $\Delta\kappa_i$, 1\textsuperscript{st} derivative of $\kappa$ \\
$f_4$ & Cum.\ dist.\ norm & $\textstyle\sum_{j<i}\!\|p_{j+1}{-}p_j\|/d_\text{tot}\in[0,1]$ \\
$f_5$ & Heading $\sin$    & $\sin(\theta_i)$, $\theta_i{=}\text{atan2}(\Delta y,\Delta x)$ \\
$f_6$ & Heading $\cos$    & $\cos(\theta_i)$, avoids $\pm\pi$ wrap \\
$f_7$ & Rel.\ position    & $i/(L{-}1)\in[0,1]$ \\
$f_8$ & Local curv.\ std  & $\text{std}(\kappa_{i-5{:}i+5})$, window\,=\,11 \\
$f_9$ & Curv.\ accel.     & $\Delta^2\kappa_i$, 2\textsuperscript{nd} derivative of $\kappa$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}}
\label{tab:features}
\end{center}
\end{table}

Channels $f_3$ (jerk) and $f_9$ (acceleration) encode curvature transition dynamics; $f_8$ detects oscillatory segments; $f_5,f_6$ provide unambiguous heading. This contextual enrichment via higher-order representations improves prediction accuracy~\cite{b17}.

\subsection{RoadTransformer Architecture}

The $(197{\times}10)$ matrix passes through (Fig.~\ref{fig:arch}):

\textbf{(1) Input projection:} $\mathbf{h}_i{=}\text{GELU}(\text{LN}(\mathbf{W}_p\mathbf{f}_i{+}\mathbf{b}_p))$, projecting 10-d to $d_\text{m}{=}128$.

\textbf{(2) CLS + positional embedding:} Learnable $\texttt{[CLS]}\in\mathbb{R}^{128}$ prepended (len.~198); positional embeddings $\mathbf{E}\in\mathbb{R}^{198\times128}$ added.

\textbf{(3) Pre-LN Transformer Encoder~\cite{b11}:} 4 layers, 8 heads, FFN~512, GELU, dropout~0.1:
\vspace{-4pt}
\begin{align}
\mathbf{Z}' &= \mathbf{Z}^{(l)} + \text{MHA}(\text{LN}(\mathbf{Z}^{(l)})), \notag\\
\mathbf{Z}^{(l+1)} &= \mathbf{Z}' + \text{FFN}(\text{LN}(\mathbf{Z}'))
\end{align}
\vspace{-6pt}

\noindent Self-attention relates all 197 positions globally.

\textbf{(4) MLP classifier:} CLS output $\to$ LN $\to$ Linear(128$\to$64) $\to$ GELU $\to$ Drop(0.2) $\to$ Linear(64$\to$1) $\to$ Sigmoid. Output $\hat{y}{\in}[0,1]$ is failure probability (${\sim}400$K params total).

\subsection{Training (SWA) and Deployment}

Adam (lr\,=\,1e-3, BCE), 75 epochs on 28,804 samples. SWA~\cite{b6} starts at epoch\,50---averaging weights over the final 25 epochs finds a flatter loss basin, improving robustness to unseen road geometries. The SWA checkpoint (\texttt{roadfury\_best.pt}) stores the averaged state dict and per-channel normalization statistics.

At inference, $n$ test cases are batched $(n,10,197)$, scored in one forward pass, sorted by descending $\hat{y}$, and streamed back via gRPC. The service implements \texttt{Name()}, \texttt{Initialize(stream Oracle)} (acknowledged; no online retraining), and \texttt{Prioritize(stream SDCTestCase)}. Launched via:
{\small\verb|docker run --rm -p 4545:4545 road-fury -p 4545|}

\section{Evaluation}

\textbf{Dataset.} SensoDat~\cite{b7}: 36,006 labeled BeamNG simulation tests (RF\,=\,1.5, max speed 120\,km/h, OOB\,=\,50\%), split 80/20 (28,804 train / 7,202 val).

\textbf{Baselines.} (1)~\textbf{Random}: uniform shuffle, APFD$\,{\approx}\,0.5$. (2)~\textbf{ITEP4SDC}: prior competition SoA---ONNX feedforward MLP, 3 features, pre-fitted scalers.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{Test Prioritization Performance}
\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{center}{\small
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Tool} & \textbf{APFD} & \textbf{\#Feats} & \textbf{Architecture} \\
\midrule
Random            & 0.493                        & --  & --              \\
ITEP4SDC          & 0.781                        & 3   & ONNX MLP        \\
\textbf{RoadFury} & \textbf{0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012} & 10  & Transformer+SWA \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}}

RoadFury achieves APFD~=~0.804 ($\sigma$~=~0.012), outperforming ITEP4SDC by \textbf{+2.9\%} and random by \textbf{+63\%}. Low $\sigma$ across trials confirms SWA's flat-basin stability.

\textbf{Key factors:}
(1)~\emph{Richer feature dynamics}---$f_3$ (jerk) and $f_9$ (acceleration) capture the onset and rate of curvature change at each road point, detecting dangerous transitions invisible to ITEP4SDC's 3 static features. $f_8$ identifies oscillatory segments where autopilots repeatedly over-steer.
(2)~\emph{Sequential attention}---ITEP4SDC processes points independently; RoadFury relates all 197 positions simultaneously, recognizing compound patterns (long straight accelerating into a hairpin) whose danger exists only in their combination. This global structural awareness directly translates to earlier fault detection and higher APFD.

%=====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}

\textbf{RoadFury} achieves APFD~=~0.804\,$\pm$\,0.012 on the ICST~2026 SDC Competition benchmark---a new state of the art over the ONNX-based ITEP4SDC baseline. By uniting a 10-channel road geometry representation, a Pre-LN Transformer Encoder, and Stochastic Weight Averaging, it captures both local curvature dynamics and global sequential structure that feedforward baselines discard. Future work includes online oracle adaptation (few-shot fine-tuning on \texttt{Initialize} oracle data) and feature-subset ablation. Tool available at: \url{https://github.com/chisngyen/sdc-test-prioritization-novel}.
